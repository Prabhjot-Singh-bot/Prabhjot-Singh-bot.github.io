Russia

In Russia, birthdays are celebrated with family and friends. The birthday person often organizes a party at home, in a restaurant, or outdoors. Guests bring gifts, flowers, and cards, while the host provides food and drinks. Traditional Russian dishes like salads, cakes, and snacks are common. People toast to the birthday person’s health, happiness, and success. It is also customary to sing a Russian version of the birthday song. For children, birthdays may include games, clowns, or magicians. Milestone birthdays, like turning 50, are celebrated more elaborately. Russians also celebrate name days, based on the Orthodox calendar of saints. This video shows that how birthdays are celebrated in Russia
